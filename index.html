Indian Food

The traditional food of India has been widely appreciated for its fabulous use of herbs and spices. Indian cuisine is known for its large assortment of dishes. The cooking style varies from region to region and is largely divided into South Indian and North Indian cuisine.

  • BENGALI
  • GUJARATI
  • MUGHLAL
  • PUNJABI
  • SOUTH INDIAN

Bengali Food

Bengali cuisine is appreciated for its fabulous use of panchphoron, a term used to refer to the five essential spices namely mustard, fenugreek seed, cumin seed, aniseed, and black cumin seed. The specialty of Bengali food lies in the perfect blend of sweet and spicy flavors.

Gujarati Food

The traditional Gujarati food is primarily vegetarian and has a high nutritional value. The typical Gujarati thali consists of varied kinds of lip smacking dishes. Gujarati cuisine has so much to offer and each dish has an absolutely different cooking style.

Mughlai Food

Mughlai cuisine is one of the most popular cuisines, whose origin can be traced back to the times of the Mughal Empire. Mughlai cuisine consists of dishes that were prepared in the kitchens of royal Mughal Emperors.

Punjabi Food

The cuisine of Punjab has an enormous variety of mouth-watering vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. The spice content ranges from minimal to pleasant to high. Punjabi food is usually relished by people of all communities.

South Indian Cuisine

South Indian cuisine is known for its light, healthy meals with a perfect balance of spices. Rice, lentils, coconut, and tamarind are widely used ingredients in South Indian dishes.
